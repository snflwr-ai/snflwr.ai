# Required User-Facing Disclosures

> **This document is not legal advice.** It consolidates the disclosures that
> should be surfaced to users (children, parents, educators) and where each
> belongs. The entity-dependent items cannot be finalized until a business
> entity is registered (see `legal/LAWYER_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md` →
> "Pre-publication blockers"). Have counsel review before relying on any of it.

The legal documents in `legal/` (Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Data
Processing Agreement) remain **DRAFT**. The disclosures below are the
short, plain-language notices that belong **in the product UI and onboarding**,
linking back to those full documents.

---

## 1. AI-generated content notice  *(ready — surface in chat UI + onboarding)*

> **snflwr.ai is an AI tutor.** Its answers are generated by an AI model and may
> be incomplete or incorrect. It is a study aid, not a replacement for a teacher,
> textbook, or a parent/guardian. Please double-check important facts.

## 2. Not professional advice / crisis resources  *(ready — surface in chat UI)*

> snflwr.ai does **not** provide medical, mental-health, legal, or emergency
> advice. If you or someone you know is struggling or in danger, talk to a
> trusted adult, teacher, or counselor, or call or text the **988 Suicide &
> Crisis Lifeline**. In an emergency, call 911.

*(This mirrors the in-conversation self-harm response already enforced by the
safety pipeline — see `safety/pipeline.py` `get_safe_response`.)*

## 3. Adult supervision / intended use  *(ready — onboarding)*

> snflwr.ai is intended for K-12 learners used **with parental or school
> supervision**. A parent, guardian, or school must set up the account and
> provide the consent described in the Privacy Policy.

## 4. Privacy & parental consent (COPPA)  *(ready — link; consent flow exists)*

> We collect limited information to operate the tutor. Children under 13 require
> verifiable parental consent before use, and parents can review or delete their
> child's data at any time. See our **[Privacy Policy](../../legal/PRIVACY_POLICY.md)**
> and **[parental consent process](./COPPA_CONSENT_MECHANISM.md)**.

## 5. No warranty / limitation of liability  *(covered in ToS — link only)*

> The service is provided "as is." See the **[Terms of Service](../../legal/TERMS_OF_SERVICE.md)**.

## 6. Terms acceptance  *(ready — onboarding)*

> By creating an account you agree to the **[Terms of Service](../../legal/TERMS_OF_SERVICE.md)**
> and **[Privacy Policy](../../legal/PRIVACY_POLICY.md)**.

## ⛔ Entity-dependent disclosures — REQUIRED by COPPA § 312.4(d)(1), BLOCKED

These are **legally required** in the privacy policy but **cannot be written
until the operating entity exists.** Do not invent them. Fill once registered
(blockers #1–#3 in `legal/LAWYER_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md`):

| Field | Required by | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Legal entity name (LLC/Corp) | COPPA § 312.4(d)(1) | ❌ no entity registered |
| Operator mailing address (PO box) | COPPA § 312.4(d)(1) | ❌ pending |
| COPPA contact phone number | COPPA § 312.4(d)(1) | ❌ pending |
| Named subprocessors (e.g. SendGrid, host) | Privacy Policy § 7.2 / DPA § 3.3 | ❌ placeholders `[Email Provider]`, `[Hosting Provider]` |
| Governing-law state | ToS § 13.1 / DPA § 13 | ❌ pending entity |

## Where each disclosure must appear

- **Chat UI (persistent footer / first message):** #1 AI-generated, #2 crisis.
- **Onboarding / account creation:** #3 supervision, #4 consent, #6 terms acceptance.
- **Privacy Policy body:** the entity-dependent block above (once unblocked).
- **Settings / About page:** links to ToS, Privacy Policy, DPA.

> **Open gap:** none of #1–#6 are surfaced in the product UI today (the chat is
> stock Open WebUI with no disclosure footer/onboarding copy). Wiring these into
> the UI is a launch blocker — see the live-readiness blocker list.
